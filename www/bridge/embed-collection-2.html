Give the animals a cup of water so they will think we are human!

Translated by Sevgim Deniz Egilmez

Turkey has lots street animals who naturally live on the streets of major cities, mostly cats and dogs. In countries like the US, we don’t see animals without owners roaming freely on the streets; there are some feral cat colonies but other than that animal control collects any stray animals off of the streets. Currently, a system like that wouldn’t be a good fit for Turkey. It may be difficult for a foreigner to understand the intricacies of this subject as it goes way back a couple hundred years, all the way to Ottoman Empire times. The brutal, inhumane and ultimately unsuccessful campaigns put in effect to eradicate the street animals in those days not only were hurtful to the animals but the people as well. Without dwelling too much into history, and the psychosocial background of the issue :) I can say that the majority of Turks prefer to live with street animals than seeing them being collected and killed off in shelters like done in the West. The current administration has tried to pass laws that would result in mass extermination of these animals but each time thankfully the public has protested and prevented such laws being introduced. I hope they are at a stand-still on the subject until they can come up with a better solution.

With all that being said, this tweet refers to a natural people’s movement of providing a cup of water and food in front of their apartment buildings, businesses etc. to help these homeless animals survive through harsh weather conditions like summer heat and snow/freezing weather in the winter. If you visit Istanbul in summer time you may see cups of water and food being left out for these animals. Whenever I go back for a visit, I personally feed and care for the street animals, and actually I have ended up rescuing a street cat two years ago and brought him back with us to theUS. There are of course always those people who think animals bring dirt and disease and so those people will throw away those cups or advise their neighbors against putting food and water out.

This Tweet is asking for people to leave a cup of water out, so at least we can fool the animals and make them believe more of us are actual human beings. This basically refers to the person acting cruel as a non-human.

—  Sevgim Deniz Egilmez
