1. Stack Building Scenario

  1. This guy Paul is walking around the planet. I want to translate all the stuff near him. I create a spreadsheet of all the things I want translated. Like this:

    ID URL Source Lang
    1 twitter.com/post/1 "Hi this is my first tweet!" en
    2 twitter.com/post/2 "... and my second tweet!" en
    3 twitter.com/post/3 "Wow this is my THIRD tweet!" en

  2. The spreadsheet is our private stack of work-jobs for translators ... I add things to the stack in various ways — I usually add small bits of social media content.

  3. Each row in the spreadsheet is a job that a translator can perform. When I find something I want translated, I add it to the spreadsheet.

2. Stacks: Tre finds translation

  1. Before you can translate something, you have to find something worth translating. For now we just do this with Gdocs.

  2. So, because I have a gmail address, Clarice just invited me to this spreadsheet. Each row is a tweet that could be translated.

  3. In this spreadsheet, not everything needs to be translated.

  4. You just choose which one you want to work on by clicking a link in the row.

3. Editing: Tre is translating

  1. After I signed up and logged in and chosen some work ... I get into this editor. It's pretty simple. I create the translation and click done.

  2. I see here I can use some basic support tools... Then I just type the translation out, and maybe read it out loud to myself ...

  3. I click "done" ... and Clarice gets it. Usually that is the last I see of the translation job, but sometimes Clarice the Client does a review of my work.

4. Embedding: Clarice publishes her Stack

  1. It's hard to read the spreadsheet.

    Once all of the translations are done by translators like Tre .. I go to my stack page and there is this little embed button ... When I click it I get these simple options for how to embed the stack.

  2. The embed is not very easy to read.

    I get the embed code and I know the embed will always look like what I am seeing here. The embed is really simple one-column view that works almost anywhere. I am presenting the result of our curation and translation.
