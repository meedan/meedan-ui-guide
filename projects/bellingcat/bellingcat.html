Identifying MH17 parts: nose & flight deck (Section 41)

0 Comments (Be the first!)
  • Share
    • Share on Facebook
    • Share on Twitter
    • Share on Google+

Malaysia Airlines flight MH17 crashed over Eastern Ukraine on Thursday July 17th, likely as a result of shrapnel damage sustained from a surface-to-air missile, and subsequent massive explosive decompression. 298 lives were lost. The debris from the wreckage spread across a large area, by some estimates about about 15 km (9 miles) in diameter.

A huge amount of reports and images of the aircraft's parts have been circulating on social media and websites. Verification, identification & geolocating is needed for the reports and related images. This story is focused on pieces of the aircraft's forward area (including the flight deck), known as 'section 41'.

Please note that the fuselage skin forward of the first doors/around the cockpit area have heavy grey residue from chemical pitting & fumes. This pitting in many locations has a darker impact area then streams away. This is a good indication of airflow at point of impact & thus helping to determine what side of the part to the top & forward vs to the rear.

Unlike the other images of the transporter, a phone number was visible on the side of the truck, a number which Paris Match called, contacting the owner of the vehicle. According to Paris Match, he claimed it was stolen from rebel controlled Donetsk, and the number belongs to this vehicle hire company, located here in Donetsk.
