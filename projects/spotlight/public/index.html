Partner Spotlight

With so much talk of the impact of the internet in the Middle East, starting a printed weekly might seem counterintuitive. But for Welad el Balad, hyperlocal weekly papers are a critical part of their work.

In a country where rumors and unverified media fly around Facebook and Twitter, print offers a path to legitimacy for the media company, founded in 2012.

One of Checkdesk’s most active partners, Welad el Balad has founded 10 local newsrooms across Egypt, each of which publish their own weekly paper. Additionally, they run a national online aggregator in partnership with leading portal Masrawy that covers 20 governorates.

“Welad El Balad was set up in 2012 with a mission to give communities access to skills and outlets that allow them to take ownership of media production at the local level,” noted Fatemah Farag, Founder and Director at Welad El Balad.

Making that mission a reality requires a lot of work—two shifts a day, seven days a week, starting at 8:30 am and running till nearly midnight. The newsrooms coordinate using Google Drive, email, chat, Facebook and telephone. In the flurry of messages, Checkdesk plays a critical role.

“For our trainees in Egypt’s governorates,” explained Farag, “the training has been key as well as developing the fact checking nature of the platform.”

“What is legitimate, what is credible, is print,” noted Fatemah Farag, Executive Editor, in a recent interview. And behind that credibility is ensuring that the facts they report are verified and accurate.

Farag and her team began using Checkdesk in 2013, and formed Yomaty.net. Tested in Cario and cities across Egypt, Yomaty has been used to investigate many rumors spreading via social media, in particular related to the ongoing transition from Muslim Brotherhood rule to the presidency of Abdelfattah El-Sisi.

Checkdesk’s viability rests not just on software but on training, and training has played a central role in Yomaty’s implementation. In 2013 alone, Welad led trainings in 7 governorates with over 800 students of journalism. They also implemented an internship program focused on the Hani Darwish Citizen Journalism Prize, which honors outstanding trainees that demonstrated advanced proficiencies in verification techniques and citizen journalism.

Print is not the organization’s only focus. Yomaty receives on average 20,000 views a month and is regularly cited across the Egyptian and Arabic-speaking web. Recently, Welad El Balad have also partnered with leading Egyptian digital news portal Masrawy to bring news from Egypt’s governorates to the a new audience.

With a mission of boosting media excellence and developing community media, Welad El Balad holds verification in the highest regard. Says Farag: “By developing independent local community media accountable to its readership at the highest professional standards we challenge in practice the highly centralized nature of media production in the country.”

Yomaty's Checkdesk is a shield for our community against rumors and fake news; it's a way to build trust between readers and news-makers. For more than 10 months we have played a major role in fact checking important news events, like election campaigns and coverage of Egypt's recent presidential elections. We've also verified statements attributed to well-known people while uncovering many fake social media accounts claiming to represent them.
— Wafaa Heikal, Yomaty Community Manager

Fatemah Farag is the Founder and Director of Welad Elbalad Media Service. She has a background in journalism with work as editor and staff reporter at a wide variety of publications such as Al Masry Al Youm, Al-Ahram Weekly, the Washington Post and the MIddle East Times. She has a Masters in political science from the American University in Cairo.
